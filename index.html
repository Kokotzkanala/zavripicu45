REASONS FOR SEIZURE: DETAILED INVESTIGATION FINDINGS

The domain https://adobecrackeddownloads.linkpc.net was a low-profile but highly dangerous piracy hub operated by a syndicate with deep ties to underground forums. Despite its small scale — only 38,412 total downloads recorded since launch in March 2025 — the site was a critical node in a larger criminal network. It distributed cracked Adobe software (e.g., Photoshop 2025 [m0nkrus], GenP patches) alongside malware-laden installers for Microsoft Office, Autodesk, and others. The site’s modest traffic was intentional: it served as a private distribution point for verified forum members and DDoS-for-hire clients.

Key Intelligence:

  • Low Download Volume (38,412 total): Site used invite-only access via BreachForums and Exploit.in. Downloads required forum reputation or $25 BTC/XMR payment. 92% of users were repeat offenders.
  • Private OSINT Assistance: Operation accelerated by ZachBTX, a renowned blockchain investigator, who provided pro bono wallet clustering and forum infiltration under FBI supervision. ZachBTX’s analysis linked 7 of 8 traced wallets to the syndicate within 72 hours.
  • ISP Cooperation: Real-time traffic mirroring enforced via court orders to T-Mobile, Verizon, AT&T, Charter Spectrum, Comcast Xfinity, Cox Communications. Over 1.8 million packets captured from U.S. IPs alone.
  • Hacking Forum Ties: Administered by IntelBroker (arrested) and affiliates. Threads on BreachForums advertised "Adobe CC 2025 + GenP" with embedded GoFile links. Private messages revealed DDoS contracts and malware sales.
  • Financial Fraud & Botnet: $50M+ laundered through fake software sales, phishing kits, and a 200,000-device botnet offering DDoS attacks at $100/hour. Monero used for 60% of transactions to evade tracing.
  • Malware Distribution: 48% of downloads contained credential stealers and crypto-miners. Infected machines were recruited into the botnet via backdoors in GenP patches.
