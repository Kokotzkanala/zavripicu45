REASONS FOR SEIZURE: DETAILED INVESTIGATION FINDINGS

The domain https://adobecrackeddownloads.linkpc.net served as the central command hub for a sophisticated international cybercrime syndicate responsible for the largest-scale unauthorized distribution of Adobe Creative Cloud software, alongside other pirated applications, in recorded history. The operation, active from January 2023 to November 2025, violated multiple international treaties, caused Adobe Inc. estimated losses exceeding $1.8 billion USD, and was deeply intertwined with underground hacking forums such as BreachForums (Forum-1), Exploit.in, and XSS.is. The syndicate's operators laundered proceeds through cryptocurrency tumblers and used the funds to finance additional criminal enterprises.

Primary Violations Identified:

  • Mass Distribution via GoFile & Torrents: Over 3.2 million cracked installers uploaded to GoFile.io under premium accounts controlled by the syndicate. Torrent files seeded across networks like The Pirate Bay, 1337x, and private trackers, including cracks for Microsoft Office 365, Autodesk AutoCAD, and CorelDRAW suites.
  • Hacking Forum Ties: Syndicate members posted cracked software and zero-day exploits on BreachForums (administered by arrested hacker IntelBroker) and Exploit.in. Threads advertised "Adobe CC 2025 Full Suite + GenP Patch" with over 500 replies, leading to 1.2 million downloads.
  • Circumvention & Malware Tools: Custom keygens, patches (GenP, AMTEmu), and hosts file modifiers distributed to bypass licensing. 42% of downloads contained malware for credential theft, crypto-mining, and backdoor access.
  • Financial Fraud & Botnet Operations: Over $50 million USD stolen via phishing kits, ransomware affiliates, and a DDoS-for-hire service powered by a 200,000-device IoT botnet. Proceeds funneled through fake e-commerce sites mimicking legitimate software vendors.
  • Broader Piracy Scope: Beyond Adobe, the syndicate distributed cracked versions of Windows 11 Pro, Adobe Substance 3D, VMware Workstation, and enterprise tools like SAP and Oracle Database, affecting 192 countries.
  • Global Reach & Impact: Victims included educational institutions, corporations, and governments. Data breaches from infected downloads exposed 2.1 million personal records.
